Why I Love (and Hate) Playing Valorant

Published on May 25, 2025 by Nicolas Viana

In this article, you’ll understand why I love Valorant — even though it often drives me crazy — and exactly what makes it so frustrating sometimes. Let’s start with the good stuff: Valorant is just fun. It’s a tactical FPS (first-person shooter) where magical agents — I like to call them "fairies with guns" — use cool abilities while shooting at each other. That mix of strategy, teamwork, and fast reflexes keeps things exciting and fresh in every match. The gameplay is awesome. Every round feels intense, and learning how to master each agent’s abilities adds a unique layer of skill. Whether I’m playing solo or with friends, I always find myself having a good time. Plus, let’s be honest — the skins are super cute. Riot really knows how to make cosmetics that make you want to play "just one more match" to earn them. But... as much as I love the game, sometimes I seriously question why I keep playing it. Here’s where the frustration kicks in: I try to improve my aim, practicing to land those perfect headshots. But then I get destroyed by four body shots, and it feels like all that effort means nothing. It’s honestly demoralizing. And don’t get me started on the smurfs — high-ranked players who use low-rank accounts to dominate easier matches. It completely ruins the experience for newer or more casual players. They crush the game just to feel good about themselves, and it's incredibly unfair. Then there are the cheaters. It’s beyond annoying when you realize someone’s using hacks to win. You can’t outplay that, no matter how good you are. And finally, the matchmaking system feels completely broken sometimes. Some games are way too easy, and others are impossibly hard. It just doesn’t feel balanced, and that kills the fun when you're either stomping the other team or getting stomped yourself. Still, even with all the rage and tilted moments, Valorant keeps pulling me back. There’s something special about it — maybe it’s the potential of getting better, the thrill of a good clutch, or just laughing with friends after a wild round. I love it, I hate it… but I can’t stop playing it.
